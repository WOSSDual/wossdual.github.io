About the WOSS Dual Olympiad

The WOSS Dual Olympiad is a Math and Computer Science Competition, held in the WOSS South Campus Cafeteria, between December 18 and December 19.

It is held on two seperate days, with two rounds within one day - the first day is for Math, where participants will be solving math problems either in the individual round, or the team round.

The second day will be focusing on Computer Science, with two divisions - Junior and Senior (Junior is easier, Senior is harder) also with an individual and a team round. Participants will be solving programming problems on DMOJ with their language of choice. Participants will be assigned problems based on their division.

Participants may participate in any or all four rounds.

Rules

  • Teams consist of one to three (1 – 3) students from White Oaks Secondary School.

  • Scientific calculators are allowed for all rounds.

    • NO graphing calculators permitted!

  • All submissions will be done electronically; please bring a charged device.

    • Submission for Mathematics Individual, Mathematics Team, and Computer Science Team Rounds will be done through Google Form on your own device.

    • Bring a computer for Computer Science Individual Round.

      • The CS Individual Round will be using DMOJ; please register if you have not already!

  • Tiebreaking for prizes for mathematics will be done fairly based on problem difficulty, calculated as the sum of (problem points) * (100% − (percent of people answered correctly)).

  • Tiebreaking for prizes for computer science will be done based on speed.

Format

Mathematics Individual


  • 50 Minutes

  • 25 Problems

    • Part A: 10 Problems, 5 Points each

    • Part B: 10 Problems, 6 Points each

    • Part C: 5 Problems, 8 Points each

  • Total: out of 150

  • No points for leaving blank

  • No penalty for incorrect answers or guessing

  • Answers are integers from 00 to 99, inclusive


Mathematics Team


  • 40 Minutes

  • 10 Problems

    • Part A: 4 Problems, 5 Points each

    • Part B: 4 Problems, 6 Points each

    • Part C: 2 Problems, 8 Points each

  • Total: out of 60

  • No points for leaving blank

  • No penalty for incorrect answers or guessing

  • Answers are integers from 00 to 99, inclusive


Computer Science Individual


  • 80 Minutes

  • 3 Problems

    • 50 points for each fully accepted submission

    • Partial points are available for some problems

  • Total: out of 150

  • Unlimited submissions allowed

  • Junior and Senior Divisions

  • Will be run with DMOJ


Computer Science Team


  • 20 Minutes

  • 10 Problems

    • 5–7 points per correct answer to a problem

  • Total: out of 60

  • Theory problems for logic, syntax, and more

  • No points for leaving blank

  • No penalty for incorrect answers or guessing

  • Answers are integers from 00 to 99, inclusive
